Design Rationale

DISCORDIA

This app turns lateral leadership frameworks into a decision rehearsal tool for realistic design leadership conflict.

Open the app →

01 · Intent

Practice before the real conversation.

Lateral leadership is difficult because the leader rarely controls the decision, the timeline, or the other person's behavior. The app is designed as a rehearsal space: users make a move, see the consequence, and receive coaching tied to a framework.

Scenario selection

The four scenarios represent common design leadership tensions: deadline pressure, critique conflict, accessibility scope cuts, and stakeholder intake bypasses.

Decision structure

Each scenario has three decision points so users can practice conflict as a sequence instead of a single perfect response.

Metric model

Trust, clarity, and integrity make tradeoffs visible. A move can protect one metric while damaging another, which mirrors real lateral leadership.

Coaching layer

Every response includes consequence, coaching, and a usable sentence so the learner can transfer the framework into a real meeting.

02 · Framework choices

Why these frameworks?

Conflict styles

Fighter, Negotiator, Diplomat, Avoider, and People Pleaser are used because they help users recognize instinctive moves and their risks.

Feedback models

SBI, AID, and Radical Candor appear in critique and stakeholder scenarios because they help users stay specific, caring, and direct.

Power and influence

French and Raven, Lukes, and Cialdini inform the scenarios where power is indirect: executive requests, PM deadlines, and engineering tradeoffs.

ABC triangle

Attitude, Behavior, and Context shape the coaching prompts so users look beyond the visible behavior and read the system around it.

Design principle The app does not reward being nice or being forceful by default. It rewards reading the situation and choosing a response that protects the work, the relationship, and the truth as much as possible.
